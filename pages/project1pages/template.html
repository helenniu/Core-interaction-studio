Exercise 1: User Discovery

Research question

How can environmental activism be more accessible and be more effective in creating change and participation?

I think I’m interested in this because I feel like environmental activism has become a very influential topic and movement currently. I think the impact of more radical activism such as strikes or fasting have large unseen effects socially and could be more inclusive to all ages, races, classes. The organization of activities could be more standardized to allow for greater involvement. For me I do want to participate in climate activism, but I don’t think choosing to miss classes that I pay for is the best way for me to participate. I think there should be many more ways to be socially involved in creating change.

User survey

are you able to access information about local environmental activism activities in your area? if so, where?

Do you think marches, strikes, clubs, are effective in creating change socially/ environmentally? how so or how not?

is environmental activism inclusive of all races, ages, classes in your opinion? how so or how not?

do you think activism on digital platforms is effective? how so or not so?

Lastly, how can environmental activism be more inclusive and effective in your opinion? (more access/organization/information)
